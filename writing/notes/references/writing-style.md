# Writing style for note content

Applies to the prose body of notes. Structural elements are exempt: frontmatter, TL;DR headings, the keyed `**title** —` form in todos.md, and the generated index.

Write plainly. Every sentence should state a fact, a decision, or a question. If a word can be removed without changing the meaning, remove it.

- Say "is", not "serves as", "represents", or "marks".
- No "It's not X — it's Y", no "The result? Y.", no "Here's the thing".
- No filler transitions: "It's worth noting", "Importantly", "Interestingly".
- No gravitas words unless load-bearing: fundamentally, crucial, robust, pivotal, leverage, delve, landscape.
- No trailing participle analysis: "...highlighting its importance", "...reflecting broader trends".
- Name the source or drop the claim. Never "experts suggest" or "it's widely known".
- Don't invent concept labels ("the supervision paradox") — describe the thing.
- Sparing em dashes; no manufactured one-line dramatic paragraphs; no "In conclusion".
- State each fact once, in the most useful place. Don't restate for emphasis.

`tropes` skill (as `tropes.md` in its skill directory; `writing/tropes/tropes.md` in this
